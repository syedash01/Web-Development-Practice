Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and principal developer of the Linux Kernel, which is the kernel for a number of operating system. He also created the version contorl system Git. Torvalds was born on December28,1969 in Helsinki, Finland. He studied computer science at the University of Helsinki, and he developed the Linux kernel as a student project in 1991.Since then, the Linux Kernel has become one of the most widely used kernel in the world, powering everything from smartphone to supercomputers.

Achievements

  • 1991-Created the Linux kernel and the version control system Git
  • 2004-Named one of the 100 most Influential People in the World" by Time Magazine in 2004
  • 2010-Received the honorary doctorate from the University of helsinki in 2010
  • 2010-Received the 2010 Millennium Technology Prize and the 2012 Free Software Foundation's Award for the Advancement of Free Software
  • 2013-Induced into the Internet Hall of Fame in 2013 for his contributions to the development of the Linux Kernel and the creation of Git
  • 2014-Induced into the Hall of Fame of the Computer History Museum in 2014
  • 2015-Received the IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2019-Received the 2019 ACM SIGOPS Hall of Fame Award
